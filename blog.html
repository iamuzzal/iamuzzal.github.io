Effect of Technology in Daily Life

Technology is the application of tools, materials and knowledge to solve problems and extend human capabilities. Technology is one of the most controversial issues in our current society. New technology always changes our life very much and takes it to a new level. It is like the new way of thinking or doing the normal things differently, better and much more faster with less hassle and at a much affordable rate. Modern technology has made it possible for the discovery of many multi-functional devices like the smartwatch and the smartphone. With all of these revolutions, technology has also made our lives easier, faster, better and more fun. Technology can affect life both positively and negatively.

Positive impacts

Technology has changed the way we learn and learn. In the past, we were not able to get the flexibility of data, information and knowledge. The school was very far from home. But today, due to technology, there are online schools. Anyone can do online degrees using the internet and computers. Today, we have mobile, internet, computer and social media content, video conferencing tools, and mobile applications to communicate with anyone from anywhere in the world ...was impossible in the past. The advantage of communication exchange is the fast, easy and fastest communication method. Technology brings families closer even when they live far apart. Technology can never take the place of real kisses and hugs, but it can enable loved ones to see each other and speak to each other, even when they live on different continents.

Negative Impacts

Vast use of technology is likely to result in poor social skills. Business meetings are held over Skype and kids chat in messenger rather than meet up with a friend in person. On the face of it, technology appears to create global network bringing people together. But in fact, this replaces real-life communication and ends in social isolation. Moreover, strong social bonds are replaced with a number of shallow “friends” in social networks. As a result, people might feel lonely and depressed.Among the most dangerous effects of technology is obesity. Being absorbed by a laptop or a tablet, people tend to snack a lot, keep late hours and exercise less. The more time you sit in front of the PC, the lesser is the blood circulation in your body, not to speak of the neck and head pain. Moreover, sedentary life is the reason why many people have a curved backbone and bad posture as a result.
